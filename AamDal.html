Bengali Aam Dal

Bengali Aam Dal, also known as Tok Dal, is a traditional Bengali lentil dish that combines the tartness of raw mangoes with the earthy flavors of lentils. It's a popular summer dish in Bengal, especially during the mango season when raw mangoes are abundant. To prepare Bengali Aam Dal, split red lentils (masoor dal) are cooked with water until they're soft and mushy. Meanwhile, raw mangoes are peeled and diced, adding a sour flavor to the dish. The lentils and mangoes are then cooked together with a blend of spices such as mustard seeds, cumin seeds, turmeric, dried red chilies, and sometimes a pinch of sugar or jaggery to balance the flavors. The dish is typically tempered with a tadka (tempering) of spices like mustard seeds, cumin seeds, dried red chilies, and curry leaves in hot oil or ghee, which is then poured over the cooked lentils. Bengali Aam Dal is usually served hot with steamed rice, accompanied by other side dishes like fried vegetables or fish curries. It offers a delightful combination of tanginess from the mangoes, spiciness from the tempering, and the comforting texture of lentils, making it a refreshing and flavorful meal, especially during the hot summer months.

Ingredients

  • Split red lentils (masoor dal)
  • Raw green mango, peeled and diced
  • Turmeric powder
  • Salt, to taste
  • Sugar or jaggery, to taste
  • Mustard oil or any cooking oil
  • Panch phoron (Bengali five-spice blend consisting of fenugreek seeds, nigella seeds, cumin seeds, black mustard seeds, and fennel seeds)
  • Dried red chilies
  • Fresh coriander leaves, chopped (for garnish)

Step by Step Instructions

  1. Rinse the split red lentils (masoor dal) under cold water until the water runs clear. In a pot, add the rinsed lentils along with diced raw green mango, turmeric powder, and salt.
  2. Add water, enough to cover the lentils and mango. Bring to a boil over medium heat, then reduce the heat and let it simmer until the lentils and mango are soft and cooked through, stirring occasionally. This usually takes about 20-25 minutes.
  3. In a separate small pan, heat mustard oil or any cooking oil. Add panch phoron (Bengali five-spice blend) and dried red chilies to the hot oil.
  4. Let the spices sizzle and release their aroma, but be careful not to burn them.
  5. Once the lentils and mango are cooked, add the prepared tempering to the pot. Stir well to combine, allowing the flavors to meld together.
  6. Taste the dal and adjust the seasoning according to your preference. Add more salt if needed.
  7. Depending on the tartness of the mango, you may want to add sugar or jaggery to balance the flavors. Adjust sweetness according to taste.
  8. Garnish the Aam Dal with freshly chopped coriander leaves.
  9. Serve hot with steamed rice or roti (Indian flatbread) for a delicious and comforting meal.
